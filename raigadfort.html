Raigad Fort

About

Raigad is a hill fort situated about 25 km from Mahad in the Raigad district and stands 2,851 feet above the sea level.
The British Gazette states the fort was known to early Europeans as the Gibraltar of the East.
Its decisive feature is a mile and a half flat top which has adequate room for buildings. In its prime, the fort had 300 stone houses and a garrison of 2,000 men.

There are approximately 1,737 steps leading to the fort.
The Raigad Ropeway, an aerial tramway, reaches 400 meters in height and 750 meters in length, and allows visitors to reach the fort from the ground in only four minutes.
Many constructions and structures on Raigad were built by Chhatrapati Shivaji and the Chief Engineer was Hiroji Indulkar.
When Chhatrapati Shivaji made it his capital in 1674 upon being crowned the king of the Maratha kingdom, which later developed into the Maratha Empire

Raigad is well surrounded by mountains and was considered secured.
The fort is surrounded on its north and east by Kaal River, and Gandhari River flows from its west side. Fort Lingana is situated to the east side and Kokandiva to the north.
If the skies are clear, we can see Rajgad-Torna on the east and Pratapgad, Vasota and Makarandagad on the south.

History

Raigad was known as the mountain of “Rairi” before it was captured by Shivaji Maharaj. Due to its impregnability,
Raigad was known as “Gibraltar of East”. In the 16th century, when this was not developed as a fort,
it was called by local people as “Rashivata” and “Tanas”.
Due to its shape of a tall oil lamp tower, it was also called as “Nandadeep”.

The fort was initially used to keep prisoners during the Nizamshahi rule.
It was then ruled by the More clan of the Javli region. On 6th April 1656, Shivaji Maharaj besieged Rairi and captured it in May.
The then Subhedar of Kalyan was transporting the treasure of Adilshah to Bijapur, which was raided by Marathas and utilized to fortify Raigad.

Raigad has been one of the most importance forts, and has been a witness of significant events – Shiv Raajabhishek (The Coronation ceremony of Shivaji Maharaj) was the most significant event in the history of India.
Shivaji Maharaj became the King of the newly formed Maratha kingdom after a struggle of more than 300 years.
Shivaji Maharaj took the blessings of Bhavani Mata of Pratapgad on 19th May 1674 and offered a golden umbrella (Chatra) to the goddess before the ceremony.
The ceremony took place on the auspicious day of 6th June 1674, which was a Saturday.

FAQs

How to reach Raigad fort?

Ans. Raigad fortis well connected with the rest of India, and can be reached using various modes of transports

By Air:

Pune airport is the nearest airport to this fort. It is about 126 km away from the fort.

By Train:

Mangoan station or Vir-Dasgaon stations on the Konkan railway are the convenient railheads to reach Raigad fort Maharashtra.
Buses and other private vehicles are available from stations till the fort.

By Road:

Raigad being situated 30km to the north of Mahad, ST buses ply regularly from Mahad. There are buses from Mumbai Central and Pune which ply to Mahad or Raigad via Mahad. We can ascend Raigad either by walk or by ropeway.
If we want to ascend the fort by walk, we can go from Chitta Darwaja or Nana Darwaja and we reach MahaDarwaja.
It takes about 2 hours to reach the top.
If we reach the top of the fort by ropeway, it takes only 15-20 minutes. However, wait time for ropeway depends on the crowd.

By Boat:

People from Mumbai can reach this fort by Boat. Motor launches are available from Gateway of India in Mumbai to Alibaug.
They take only 45 minutes to reach Alibaug. Then you can catch private vehicles from there till Raigad.


What is the best time to go for the Raigad Fort Trek?

Ans. The months between November to March are considered to be the best time to enjoy a session of Raigad Fort Trek. Around this time, the climatic conditions are comparatively more flourished and comfortable, making it easy for you to enjoy a hassle-free hiking experience. 


How long does it take to climb Raigad Fort?

Ans. The climb to the top takes up around 3 hours to get complete after which you are provided with some of the most enchanting views of the surroundings. In addition to this, the whole trek lets you pass through the scenic areas of the forests and fortresses, therefore making it an easy as well as eye-catching experience.
